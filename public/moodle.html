Social Engineering

What is social engineering?

Social Engineering is the use of manipulation and/or deception in order to force a target divulge personal or confidential information and or perform an action.

Web attacks

Web attacks are malicious websites that pretend to be something they are not.This can range from a site that wants to steal your log in information to a site that automatically downloads malicious file for you to click on.
In (fig 3.1) There some highlighted things to look out for.
Highlighted in red is the URL , is this the right webpage you visited. It could be only something small like a character missing or an extra one put in or even a letter from a different alphabet that looks similar like in go√≥gle.com the second 'o' is from a different alphabet.

Always check to see if there is a green lock when visiting a webpage and be extra cautious if you are on a webpage that is unsecure.

USBs & Removeable Devices

Removable Devices Attacks

Removable devices such as USBs are dangerous tools that are deployed by attackers.An attacker can leave a devices like a USB lying around hoping for someone to pick it up and plug it into their computer. These removable devices can have malware inside of them that runs as soon as you plug the device into your computer. Another method they deploy is embedding malware into a file that entices the victims curiousity making them want to click onto the file ,but when this file is clicked malware runs. Removeable devices from unknown sources should never be picked up and inserted into ones computer.
